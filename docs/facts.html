What is Carbon Footprint?

A carbon footprint is the total greenhouse gas emissions caused by our actions. It is calculated by summing the emissions generated from each stage of a product's lifecycle.

Reduce carbon footprint with these simple steps

Regulate your home temperature

Home heating and cooling temperatures accounts for 40 percent of household energy consumption. During winters, snuggle under a blanket and try to minimize the use of electric heaters and comforters or heat the rooms that are in use for a short time period. During summers, avoid layered clothing, take out window shades to block sunlight.

Consider green transport in terms of travelling

Every litre of unleaded gas consumed produces approximately 2.5 kg* of greenhouse gas. Consider carpooling as an alternative to driving, save running errands and execute them in one trip, for nearby destinations, consider walking an extra mile or use public transport even if it is once a week.

Switch off household appliances

Many standby household appliances use as much as 90 percent of their usual capacity and wasted energy from appliances left on standby is responsible for 4 million tonnes of excess carbon dioxide each year. By switching off appliances that are not in use and replacing older appliances with the ones with a 5 star rating we can save energy.
